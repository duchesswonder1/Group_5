OUR HISTORY

St. Mary's School was founded in 1906 by Reverend Father Raux Modeste of the White Fathers Congregation and later became St. Mary's College Kisubi (SMACK), now owned by the Catholic Archdiocese of Kampala. Initially established in Rubaga to provide quality education for Catholic youths, the school evolved from a lower primary section to a college offering commercial training in 1922 and later advanced to a higher school status after 1956. Due to limited space at Rubaga, the school relocated to Kisubi in 1924, on land granted by Kabaka Mutesa I. The first students excelled academically, securing all five available scholarships to Makerere College in 1923. In 1926, administration transitioned from the White Fathers to the Brothers of Christian Instruction from Canada. By 1936, SMACK had introduced the Cambridge School Certificate (C.S.C), further solidifying its reputation as a top academic institution in Uganda.

Our Library

Knowledge is Power – Explore, Learn, and Grow

Welcome to the SMACK Library

The library at St. Mary's College Kisubi (SMACK) plays an important role in promoting academic excellence and supporting the school's mission of providing quality education. It serves as a central hub for learning and research, offering students an environment that encourages intellectual growth and academic exploration. Through its wide range of resources and facilities, the library helps learners acquire knowledge and develop essential research skills needed for their studies.

One of the major strengths of the library is the extensive collection of books, journals, and digital resources that cater to various subjects taught at both O-level and A-level. The institution provides students with access to valuable learning materials that support their curriculum and enhance their understanding of different disciplines. In addition, the library offers well-designed spaces that accommodate both the individual and group study sessions, ensuring that students have a comfortable and conducive atmosphere for learning.

The library also emphasises the integration of technology into education. Equipped with modern computers and high-speed internet connectivity, it enables students toconduct online research efficiently and access digital learning materials with ease. These technological facilities improve the quality of learning by exposing students to up-to-date information and providing opportunities for independent study and academic advancement.

The Higher School Certificate (HSC) Library specifically serves students in the senior secondary section and is dedicated to meeting their advanced academic needs. It was officially opened by the late Hon. Namirembe Butamazire, who was the Minister of State in charge of primary education at that time. The establishment of this library was intended to enrich the learning experience of advanced-level students by providing them with specialised resources and a suitable environment for higher academic achievement.

Another important facility is the SMACK Memorial Library, which holds both historical and educational significance. Established in 1964 and commissioned by Lord Snowdon, the library was built in memory of twelve students who tragically died in a motor accident. It stands as a symbol of the college's resilience and commitment to preserving its rich history. Besides serving as a tribute to the fallen students, the Memorial Library continues to support the school's academic programs by providing students and staff with a large collection of books, journals, and other learning materials, thereby contributing significantly to the educational development of the institution.
