What does "Duc in Altum" mean? ▸

"Duc in Altum" is Latin for "launch into the deep." It comes from the Gospel of Luke, where Jesus tells Peter to row into deep water and cast his nets. For SMACK, it means pushing past comfort and mediocrity to reach for the highest standard — in faith, studies, and character.

About Us

St. Mary's College Kisubi was founded in 1906 by Rev. Fr. Raux Modeste, a missionary of the White Fathers. The school later moved to its present site at Kisubi along the Kampala–Entebbe Road, where it has stood for over a century.

Tap to read our full history ▸

Known by the nickname "SMACK" and the motto Duc in Altum, the College is owned by the Archdiocese of Kampala and has produced generations of doctors, lawyers, clergy, and leaders across Uganda.

1906 — Founded by Fr. Raux Modeste.
1918 — SMACKOBA, the Old Boys' Association, is formed.
1924 — The College moves to its permanent site at Kisubi.
2006 — SMACK celebrates 100 years of "launching into the deep."
